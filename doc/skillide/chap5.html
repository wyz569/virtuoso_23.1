Workspaces in SKILL IDE

SKILL IDE lets you configure user interface components to suit your individual work preferences. This customized configuration of toolbars and assistants is called a workspace.

Each workspace in SKILL IDE is designed to help you perform a set of related tasks, such as checking, coding, and debugging. The workspace can be tailored to a particular application, cellview, or work objective.

You can choose to either use the available workspaces or create your own workspace while working in the SKILL IDE window.

You can even customize SKILL IDE workspaces on requirement basis. You can use a workspace to change the way you view your data by choosing what assistant panes and toolbars appear in your window, where they appear, and whether each assistant pane is docked or floating. You cannot use a workspace to manipulate (filter or reduce) the underlying data itself.
